Fascinating Facts About Space

Earth as a 'Pale Blue Dot' In 1990, NASA's Voyager 1 spacecraft captured a photograph of Earth from a distance of about 6 billion kilometers (3.7 billion miles). In the image, Earth appears as a tiny dot, emphasizing the vastness of the universe and our planet's smallness within it.

Crystal Ball Nebula Revealed by JWST The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has provided unprecedentedly detailed images of the Crystal Ball Nebula (NGC 1514), located 1,500 light-years away. The nebula's intricate gas clouds and rings resemble an hourglass or ring structure, offering new insights into stellar evolution.

Jupiter's Great Red Spot Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a massive storm larger than three Earths combined, persisting for over 400 years. This persistent storm is a prominent feature of Jupiter's atmosphere.

Gamma-ray bursts release more energy in 10 seconds than our Sun will emit in its entire 10-billion-year lifetime.
