What is the smallest planet in the the milky way?

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Milky Way. It has a diameter of 4,879 kilometers, three times smaller than Earth's. It is so small that even Ganymede, a satellite of Jupiter and the largest in the Solar System, at 5,268 km in diameter, is larger than Mercury.

How are planets made?

1.Grains of dust smaller than a human hair emerge from the giant disk of gas and dust that circles young stars. 2.Gravity and collisions cause material within the disk to collide and form larger masses. 3.These larger masses attract more particles via gravity, eventually forming planets. 4..Terrestrial planets, like Earth, are primarily made of rocky material and have solid surfaces, with layers including the core, mantle, crust, and atmosphere.
